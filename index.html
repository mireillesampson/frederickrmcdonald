Artist Biography

Frederick R McDonald is an international, award-winning artist - a painter, poet and photographer - and a member of the Fort McKay First Nation. Fred was born in Fort McMurray and raised in the bush along the Athabasca River, he was brought up in the traditional hunting and trapping lifestyle of his parents. Although he has traveled far and wide, Fred’s heart is still with his community and he continues to be an active member of the Fort McKay band. Fred keeps himself grounded through his family; his children and his grandchildren are his inspiration for everything he does and they are ultimately his greatest creation.

The Indigenous Art Collection, housed at the Indigenous Art Centre, is "The most important living art collection of contemporary Indigenous art in Canada." Fred’s art has been exhibited at the IAC and his painting Dancing on a Table is held in the permanent collection.

The Glenbow Museum, the Alberta Arts Foundation and the University of Calgary all hold works by Fred in their collections. Many public art institutions and events have exhibited Fred’s paintings, including: Wheelwright Museum in Santa Fe (New Mexico, US), the Art Gallery of Alberta, Glenbow Museum, Alberta Biennial of Contemporary Art, Nickel Arts Museum, Banff Centre for Arts and Creativity and the Edmonton Art Gallery.

Fred's art is also held in many corporate and private collections and he is regularly commissioned to create paintings that bring to life the patron's vision.

"I know Fred in many different relationships. As an artist he painted the 4 foot X 6 foot memorial for Suncor’s environmental success of restoring the first oil sands mine to pristine conditions and he successfully incorporated both the historical value of the site and the future value of our recovery efforts into his images.

I know Fred as a business leader from his time as General Manager of the Fort McKay Group. We worked together on many difficult tasks and his work was always far above expectations. I know Fred as a community leader from his time as a board member with the United Way of Fort McMurray where he also served as the annual campaign char when UWFMM set a new Canadian national record for contributions per person. And I know Fred as a friend, where once again, every part of being Fred’s friend has exceeded my expectations."

Fred is a Creative Powerhouse

Since 1993, Fred has exhibited in public museums and commercial galleries, internationally.

Fred was first inspired to become a painter after he first saw the art of Arthur Schilling, an Ojibway Canadian painter.

Fred is returning to his first medium, photography, as another method to explore our world.

Inspiring and fascinating - poetry is Fred's favourite media, especially the classics: Robert Frost, WB Yeats, EA Poe, E Pauline Johnston.
