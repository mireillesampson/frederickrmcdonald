Paintings Held in Museums and Public Collections

The Indigenous Art Collection, housed at the Indigenous Art Centre, is "The most important living art collection of contemporary Indigenous art in Canada." Fred’s art has been exhibited at the IAC and his painting Dancing on a Table is held in the permanent collection.

The Glenbow Museum, the Alberta Arts Foundation, the University of Calgary and the Red Deer Museum all hold works by Fred in their collections. Many public art institutions and events have exhibited Fred’s paintings, including: Wheelwright Museum in Santa Fe (New Mexico, US), the Art Gallery of Alberta, Glenbow Museum, Alberta Biennial of Contemporary Art, Nickel Arts Museum, Banff Centre for Arts and Creativity and the Edmonton Art Gallery.

Contact Fred to arrange a commission or purchase.

cuts to the bone

"Fred's work captures the essence of the bush economy in the homelands of the Fort McKay people in the Northeastern Alberta. He paintings with a chromatic true vision that cuts to the bone. The narrative spine of his work is like the Athabasca River, rich in content, fast-flowing, and never ending"

Paintings Held in Corporate Art Collections

Suncor Energy Inc, Syncrude Canada Ltd, Birch Mountain Enterprises and Albian Sands Ltd have all trusted Fred with commissions and purchases. Such commissions depict how the companies see their relationship within the Aboriginal community and how they envision the future.

Fred collaborates with companies to communicate their vision in a way that is respectful as well as creative, often bridging past and future.

Paintings Held in Private Collections

Fred’s art has been sought-after by many private art collectors, including those who have entrusted him with commissions.

High-flying businessman David Tuccaro owns multiple paintings by Fred, including a commissioned portrait. Fred also painted the portrait of art collector, Dr John Olberg. Former President and CEO of the Glenbow Museum, Mike Robinson, not only purchased paintings for the museum, but also for his private collection.

Like most artists, Fred spends his days (or rather, nights) toiling in front of an easel. When you finally get to see the work, you immediately develop an emotional, even spiritual connection to it. That is the thing that cannot be explain by technique or tools nor can it even be taught. It simply is.

Contact Fred to arrange a commission or purchase.

"what it feels like to be home"

I first fell in love with Fred’s work in 1997. I was new in town, having arrived in Fort McMurray just a few months earlier, and I saw an announcement for an exhibition opening.

When I attended the opening I was blown away. Portraiture has long been a favourite art subject for me and I had never seen anything like Fred’s portraits. The figures are inhabiting their world. These paintings remind me of what it feels like to be home.

Since then I have travelled the world, visiting countless museums and galleries - Fred is still my favourite artist and his work still makes me feel at home anywhere I am.

Mireille Sampson
